Justworks Enterprise Design HR Tech

Employer of Record
Cost Calculator

Designing a self-service calculator that cut report generation from 1 hour to 5 seconds — and boosted EOR conversions by 15%.

Justworks is a leading HR services platform that simplifies payroll, benefits, compliance, and support for thousands of small to medium-sized businesses worldwide. My mandate was to design tools that make international hiring as seamless as domestic operations — supporting Justworks' expansion beyond the US.

Role
Product Designer
Company
Justworks
Domain
Enterprise · HR Tech
Type
Internal Tool + Client-Facing
The Problem

Sales reps spent an hour building every cost report — manually

Sales representatives were spending approximately one hour manually constructing country-specific cost reports in spreadsheet software for each client inquiry — locating tax rates, correcting formula errors, and managing version inconsistencies.

The knock-on effects were significant: prolonged sales cycles, inconsistent deliverables that undermined credibility, and lost prospects due to delayed responses. Clients also had no way to self-serve — every quote required human effort.

1 hr
to generate one cost report manually
5/day
support emails from pricing confusion
3+
disconnected tools used per report
Research & Discovery

I did the job myself first

To build real empathy, I personally executed the manual cost report process from start to finish — locating tax rates across fragmented data sources, navigating formula vulnerabilities, and piecing together a client-ready output without a standard format.

I then conducted interviews across Sales, Finance, and prospective clients, mapping workflows with emotional markers to capture both procedural friction and the frustrations underneath it.

"Both internal and external users valued speed and trust above all else."
Fragmented data sources forced reps to visit multiple systems per report
No standardized output meant every deliverable looked different
Clients had zero visibility into cost breakdowns before committing
Formula errors in spreadsheets created trust issues downstream
Design Process

From workshops to high-fidelity in four steps

01
Ideation workshops
Ran two-stage workshops — first with the design and product team for broad concept generation, then a cross-functional session with Engineering, Sales, and Finance to assess impact vs. effort and define the MVP scope.
02
Hand-drawn wireframes with annotations
Full-width low-fidelity wireframes addressed form field placement, expandable section architecture, and primary CTA positioning — enabling layout validation before committing to higher fidelity. Engineering flagged technical constraints early.
03
Mid-fidelity prototypes in Figma
Two grayscale mockups with blue accent elements enabled rapid iteration on hierarchy and flow before advancing to full Justworks design system fidelity.
04
High-fidelity + usability testing
Responsive layouts for desktop and mobile with visual distinction between employer and employee cost segments. Usability testing showed users could generate reports in under 5 seconds.
Results

700 hours saved per month. 90% fewer support emails.

The calculator delivered immediate, accurate employment expense estimates by country — covering salary, taxes, and benefits — accessible to both sales reps and clients through self-service.

Whole Foods Market Enterprise Design Retail

Grocery Data
Management

Creating a centralized source of truth for suppliers — eliminating 20 hours of weekly data reconciliation across 10+ disconnected platforms.

Whole Foods Market is a leading natural and organic grocery retailer. My mandate was to design tools that simplify grocery data management for suppliers — replacing a fragmented ecosystem of disconnected systems with one centralized, trustworthy platform.

Role
Product Designer
Company
Whole Foods Market
Domain
Enterprise · Retail Operations
Scope
End-to-End Product Design
The Problem

Data scattered across 10+ platforms was costing teams 20 hours a week

Category Managers and Procurement teams were spending up to 20 hours every week reconciling data across Excel, Smartsheet, ICon, SNIP, and more. Each platform held a different version of the truth — leading to discrepancies, eroded trust, and supplier relationships strained by delays and errors.

Competitive analysis showed that integrated platforms could reduce discrepancies by 60% and improve operational efficiency by approximately 30%. The opportunity was clear — but the execution had to be right for three distinct user groups with very different needs.

10+
disconnected platforms in the workflow
20 hrs
spent weekly on data reconciliation
3
distinct user groups with different needs
Research & Discovery

38 users. Four methods. One clear pattern.

I conducted contextual inquiries, workflow shadowing, structured interviews, and surveys with 15 Category Managers, 3 Procurement team members, and 20 suppliers — mapping real tasks against the systems they currently used.

"Users prioritized accuracy and speed over feature depth — as long as it reduced touchpoints."
All three user groups (GDO, Category Merchants, Senior Merchants) needed role-based navigation — not one-size-fits-all
Manual reconciliation created distrust: teams couldn't tell which system held the correct data
Supplier relationships were suffering from delays caused by internal inefficiency, not supplier error
Early wireflow testing showed a 40% reduction in time for core tasks before a single line of production code was written
Design Process

Five phases from architecture to QA

01
Information architecture
Developed role-based navigation for GDO, Category Merchants, and Senior Merchants — consolidating workflows and removing redundant cross-system steps.
02
Wireflow testing
Early prototypes validated with real users before high-fidelity investment. Results showed 40% reduction in time to complete core tasks.
03
Design system workshops
Collaborated with Amazon designers to adopt the Meridian Design System while maintaining Whole Foods' distinct brand identity throughout.
04
High-fidelity design
Responsive layouts built in Figma for desktop and mobile — covering offer submission, approval workflows, and supplier-facing views.
05
Development partnership & QA
Delivered annotated component specs and participated directly in QA to ensure design intent was preserved through implementation.
Results

40% faster. One source of truth. Teams freed for higher-value work.

Centralizing data across previously disconnected systems eliminated discrepancies and gave all three user groups a single, trustworthy view. Automation replaced the manual reconciliation that had consumed teams' time every week.

Ledn Product Design Fintech · Crypto

B2X Lending
Platform

Making crypto-backed lending transparent and trusted — reducing loan abandonment by 35% and growing platform usage by 40%.

Ledn is a digital asset platform offering crypto-backed loans, savings products, and other financial services to help clients manage cryptocurrency holdings. My mandate was to design lending products that simplify borrowing against volatile crypto assets — making the process intuitive, transparent, and trusted by both individual and institutional clients.

Role
Product Designer
Company
Ledn
Domain
Fintech · Crypto Lending
Audiences
Retail + Institutional
The Problem

The original platform was functional but created more questions than confidence

Users lacked visibility into how market volatility affected their collateral value and loan-to-value ratios. The absence of clear explanations discouraged borrowing — especially among first-time users making high-stakes financial decisions. A one-size-fits-all interface also failed both retail and institutional clients whose needs were fundamentally different.

Poor transparency: users couldn't track how collateral value fluctuated against loan health
Low confidence: unclear terms discouraged first-time borrowers from completing applications
Inefficient flows: confusing navigation increased loan application drop-off rates
Institutional misalignment: advanced users needed more controls, retail users needed more guidance
10+ min
to understand loan terms on old platform
High
loan application drop-off before redesign
2
distinct audiences with opposing needs
Research & Discovery

Clarity and guidance were the deciding factors

I conducted user interviews with retail and institutional clients, ran usability testing sessions observing navigation and application flows, and benchmarked competitor lending platforms in both crypto and traditional finance.

"Users needed to understand what they were risking before they could trust the platform enough to borrow."
Retail users struggled most with collateral fluctuation — they needed real-time, plain-language explanations
Institutional clients needed advanced reporting and bulk controls without sacrificing speed
Margin call warnings were critical but buried — users often didn't see them until too late
Design Process

Transparency-first design with segmented experiences

01
Guided loan application journey
Established a step-by-step application flow with contextual help, tooltips, and FAQs — removing unnecessary steps and replacing jargon with plain-language summaries.
02
Segmented dashboards
Created separate dashboard experiences for retail and institutional clients — each optimized for their specific workflows, risk tolerance, and information needs.
03
Real-time collateral visualization
Dynamic charts showing collateral performance relative to loan health, with proactive margin call alerts and clear threshold indicators.
04
Prototype testing & iteration
Tested interactive prototypes with first-time borrowers and institutional users. Feedback drove alert banners for price swings, clearer margin-call thresholds, and expanded institutional reporting panels.
05
Cross-functional go-live
Partnered with Engineering, Legal, Compliance, and Product Marketing — maintaining shared specs and coordinating launch with Support and Operations teams.
Results

Loan abandonment down 35%. Platform usage up 40%.

The redesign strengthened Ledn's competitive position through a combination of unmatched transparency and improved usability — turning a complex financial product into one users could understand and act on with confidence.

+40%

Flybits B2B SaaS AI Platform

Global
Navigation

Unifying two fragmented platforms into one — making key tasks 35% faster and cutting navigation-related support tickets by 40%.

Flybits is a B2B platform enabling financial institutions to engage customers through data-driven delivery and personalized recommendations. My mandate was to merge Flybits' two core platforms — Experience Studio (for marketers) and the Developer Portal (for developers) — into a unified navigation that made workflows seamless and features easy to find for both audiences.

Role
Product Designer
Company
Flybits
Domain
B2B SaaS · AI Platform
Clients
Major Financial Institutions
The Problem

Two platforms. One broken workflow. Users switching constantly to get anything done.

The original setup forced users to switch between Experience Studio and the Developer Portal to complete single campaigns — fragmenting workflows and burying frequently-used tools. Power users needed deep customization while casual users wanted simplicity, and the existing navigation served neither well.

Operational inefficiency slowed campaign launches and drove up support demand — bank digital teams couldn't self-serve, meaning every configuration required engineering intervention.

Fragmented workflows required platform switching to complete single tasks
Frequently used tools were buried — high-traffic features had low discoverability
Power users and casual users had opposing needs — neither were well served
Banks couldn't independently configure or launch experiences without engineering help
2
separate platforms users had to navigate
15
stakeholder interviews conducted
High
support ticket volume from navigation confusion
Research & Discovery

Two distinct user types with opposite needs

The team conducted 15 stakeholder interviews across client success, bank customers, and internal product management — supplemented by usage analytics identifying high-traffic features and direct observation sessions validating pain points.

"Power users wanted deep configurability. Casual users wanted speed, simplicity, and minimal clicks to action."
Usage analytics revealed a small set of features driving the majority of navigation — these needed to be front and centre
Observation sessions confirmed that context-switching between platforms was the single biggest source of frustration
Bank support teams validated that self-serve capability was the highest-priority outcome for their clients
Design Process

"Simple first, scalable later" — four core workflows

01
Information architecture redesign
Established clear top-level categories reducing cognitive load, with specific filters and sorting options (Category, Date, Location, Notification type, Trigger method) — streamlining redundant steps out of core workflows.
02
Four primary workflows defined
Event discovery, search, experience creation, and template use — these became the backbone of the navigation strategy, ensuring the most common paths were always one or two clicks away.
03
Interactive prototype testing
Tested with bank customer support teams — incorporating feedback on clarity and speed. Iterated on the balance between power-user depth and casual-user simplicity.
04
Design system integration
High-fidelity designs anchored critical workflows in persistent global navigation, met WCAG accessibility standards for contrast, keyboard navigation, and touch targets, and balanced marketer and developer priorities throughout.
Results

35% faster. 40% fewer support tickets. Banks can now self-serve.

The unified navigation enabled bank digital teams to independently configure and launch experiences without engineering intervention — creating genuine scalability across desktop and mobile platforms for the first time.

+20%
